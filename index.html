Introduction

Inner meditation is a particularly beneficial practice. It concentrates inward, not outside, namely, it focuses your attention and awareness on internal places of your body. This contrasts to some meditation styles that require music or imagination of external images. In inner meditation, you look at, listen to, and feel the insides of you own self by applying few basic techniques in concert, including breath, concentration, relaxation, good body posture, and mental power.

Bringing all attention inward triggers profound changes in the body and the brain. It develops and improves our energy flow, also known as qi (pronounce chee). The development of qi within yourself enhances health, generates a sense of calm and well-being, and replenishes inner energy reserves. Further, it develops awareness and consciousness based on improved body conditions, and this leads you along on a path of spiritual enlightenment.

Benefits

People start to feel physical and mental changes as healthy benefits after they practice inner meditation because this practice has healing effects in nature. Some people even begin to heal others. Here is some commonly seen benefits.

Physiologically

  • Healing effects
  • Improved health status
  • Improved sleep quality
  • Having better memory
  • Having more energy, less or no fatigue

Psychologically

  • Having more love and happy feeling
  • Calming down more easily
  • Concentrate more easily
  • Becoming more optimistic and positive
  • Becoming more open and tolerant
  • Having fewer or no complaints
  • Having fewer or no dramatic emotional changes 
Besides having improved physical and mental status, many experienced practitioners start to feel deep connections among all living beings and perceive subtle energy interactions between living beings and their environments.

Art of Practice

Inner meditation is an art of practice. You must practice it to get its benefits. Its goal is improving the body and the brain, and the only way is exercising your body and brain with certain disciplines. Inner meditation is all about practicing such disciplines.
